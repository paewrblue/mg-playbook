1The life of a game — where every term lives

Act 1 · The sleep

Dormancy

Everything is measured in turnover = price × volume = baht traded per day (baht, not shares — the operator's math is in baht). Dormancy = the median daily turnover of the year before (median = one-day spikes can't fake it). Deep-dormant < ฿5M/day is the best starting condition we measured: games born from deep dormancy paid +42%/ride vs +3.6% for already-liquid names. When a dead stock suddenly trades, there is only one kind of buyer it can be.
Act 2 · The awakening

The radar (watch-only, never an entry)

Awakening = 20-day turnover ≥5× the dormant-year median while price is still quiet (−15%…+15% over 20 days) and the new turnover is already game-worthy (≥฿2.5M/day). The extreme case we caught live: a construction name went ฿43K/day → ฿4.5M/day — a 104× awakening — with price still flat. Deep-dormant pings: 60% hit +20% within 6 months, and 53% produce a confirmed ignition ~23 trading days later. That month is the radar's product — time to do the fundamental homework. Buying the ping itself was tested: it loses to waiting.

Execution — the reality tax, measured (1,955 rides)
The signal prints after the close → enter at the next open. Measured cost ≈ 1 point/trade, win rate and multibagger count unchanged — ignition signals do not gap (median overnight gap +0.0%; the operator walks price, he doesn't jump it). Skip any entry gapping >+5% over the signal close (25 of 1,955 cases — free insurance), and work size at ≤10% of the day's turnover; bigger orders scale over days or demand a bigger game.
Act 4 · The ride

Game clock · legs · divergence · OBV

  • Game clock — the game's birthday: the day 20d turnover holds above max(1.5× dormant median, ฿2.5M/day). The absolute floor matters — a game needs game-worthy turnover in baht, not a relative blip off a dead baseline.
  • Legs — markup waves: each run to a new high separated by a ≥10% pullback. More legs = older game.
  • Divergence — the operator's exit fingerprint: a higher price peak on a later leg with weaker RSI/MACD momentum. Each push costs him more and moves price less — he is selling into his own rallies.
  • OBV (on-balance volume) — the sponsorship meter: a running total that adds volume on up-days, subtracts it on down-days. OBV rising with price = real money staying. Price up while OBV is flat = hollow markup — fragile; the crowd churns but nobody accumulates.

Act 5 · The exit

The rider exit — no profit target, ever

The operator's +20–30% markup math screens games; it is never our exit — we ride the whole trend. Three tripwires only:
  • −25% close-based disaster stop — wide enough to survive operator shakeouts (the median winning trade dips only −5.8% first; the deepest survivable shakeouts bottom at −20…−25%), tight enough to refuse game-deaths (−40…−90%). Tighter stops donate the position to the operator at the low.
  • EMA50 trail, armed at +15% — arming sells nothing; from then on, 2 consecutive closes below the EMA50 = the rally is over. This is what captures the +381%-class campaign legs that every profit target amputates.
  • Phase overrides — an ACTIVE DISTRIBUTION read, or LATE + divergence: the operator leaving IS the trend ending.
Rotation ladder (Thai names): 3 months = review only · 6 months = rotate if flat (tested: costs nothing, frees capital) · 12 months = the leash. A valuation target hit or the awaited catalyst printing = review triggers, never reflex-sells — the question is not "has the news arrived?" but "is the operator leaving on it?"
Profit brackets — tested on 1,955 rides, buried
"Take the ride to +50%, then leave at the distribution phase" fired 5 times in 1,955 rides, with outcomes identical to the trail — pressure never formally decays before price breaks the EMA50. The trail and the distribution read are the same event seen from two sides. Hard caps at 2x/3x fail on both metrics that matter: expectancy roughly halves AND return per year of capital deployed falls (capped rides are few and late — no recycling gain). The edge lives in a +300–1,700% ultra-tail that every cap amputates. Cap the clock, never the price — the rotation ladder already enforces "Thai businesses are temporary" on the time axis; a GULF/DELTA-class durable business earns a structural hold at the 1-year review through the business lens, not through an uncapped-vs-capped rule.

Game-aware technicals (not textbook)
RSI > 70 = STRENGTH in a markup, not a sell — sell the divergence, never the overbought. RSI < 45 = games rarely resume without a new ignition. EMA20 = the operator's markup rail (healthy games hold it ≥70% of days; a break on running volume = the exit bell). EMA10 catches half of all winner shakeout lows — the first 2 weeks after entry are the shakeout window: expect it, don't react to it.

What this means
The detector alone is worth ~nothing; the funnel is everything. A screener is a radar, never a signal service. The liquidity regime is the single biggest lever (~21 points per trade): when the tide turns, the correct number of new games is zero. Run order: liquidity regime → sector rotation → dormancy/ignition → informed money → the fundamental gate. The exit parameters sit on a plateau (stop 20/25/30, trail EMA30/50/100 all land within a point on identical entries) — the edge comes from selection, never from exit tuning.

The buzz clock in one line
Crowd buzz arriving on a name you're riding = your exit liquidity is assembling. Not an instant sell — it starts a clock: expect the remaining upside inside ~one quarter, treat the buzz quarter as LATE-stage input, and sell strength with the crowd, never after it.

5Hard rules & the graveyard

Hard rules
1 · No position in a pure money game — no researched fundamental case, no entry   2 · No entry while on SET Cash Balance / Trading Alert   3 · Never chase markup >20% above the approved zone   4 · A distribution read on a held name = trim review the same week   5 · ฿50M is a floor — sub-scale games are retail roulette   6 · Every call is written down, dated, and scored later
Tested and rejected — stays dead
Tight stops / EMA20 trails (operator shakeouts harvest them) · averaging down at −10% (adverse selection) · profit targets and hard caps, including banking the operator's +30% and 2x/3x brackets (the tail pays for everything) · valuation-stretch & sell-the-news auto-exits (a +381% ride looks "farfetched" the whole way up — in a money game the rally is SUPPOSED to exceed sane valuation; that's the product) · forced 3-month rotation · preferring the pullback entry · the ฿50M-in-5-days worthiness window (peak burn is a LATE-game property — ฿50M/week on a dormant name means the marketing already worked; the gate halves the deep-dormant edge and drops the single best class, the gradual-burn games: +8.4%/trade, 9% multibaggers, shallowest shakeouts) · Fibonacci retracements (winner retracements scatter 0–67%; no clustering at 38.2/50/61.8) · Elliott Wave as formal method (unfalsifiable; its useful core — leg counting and divergence-across-peaks — is already mechanical in the game clock)
Two methodology laws the calibration itself taught us
1 · Small samples lie. Twice, a 30-name smoke test showed the OPPOSITE of the full-universe result. Nothing enters this playbook on less than the full ~860-name universe — full-universe or it didn't happen. 2 · The playbook is forward-validated. Every fresh signal is logged at the next open against a control group of non-qualifying ignitions and replayed weekly under the live exit rules; if the live funnel stops beating its control, recalibration triggers automatically. A backtest is a hypothesis — the forward book is the referee.
